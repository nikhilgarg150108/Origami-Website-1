Did You Know?

  • Camels are also known as the "Ship of the Desert" as they are an effective means of transport in the sandy areas.
  • Camels have adapted very well to the hot desert climate. They store food and water in their hump and utilize it if there is scarcity of water.
  • Camels have padded feet so that they don't sink in the sand. Their eyes are well-protected against the blowing sand.

Did You Know?

  • Pigeons have an excellent navigational skill. Before telecommunication and postbox were invented, pigeons were often used to carry messages and letters.
  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years. The first recordings of this date back to Mesopotamis, modern Iraq, in 3000 BCE.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

Did You Know?

  • Male cicadas sing to attract the females. They are one of the loudest insects in the world. Some sing as loudly as a power saw, which is enough to cause permanent hearing loss if it was right next to your ear.
  • Cicada nymphs dring sap using a 'rostrum'. A rostrum is like 2 straws in 1. The cicada spits saliva down 1 straw to break up and dissolve tree roots, then uses the other straw to suck it back up.
  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect life spans. An annual cicada can live between two and five years, and a periodical cicada can live for up to 17 years in the larva stage.

Did You Know?

  • The teddy bear toy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  • A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.
  • Older teddy bears, that were created in the early 20th century, actually looked like real bears! They had long noses, dark eyes, and were very bear-shaped.
